Some online purchases require extra verification through 3D Secure (sometimes called Verified by Visa or Mastercard SecureCode). When this happens, you'll need to approve the transaction in the New Expensify app before it can go through.

# How to approve a 3D Secure transaction for your Expensify Card

When an online merchant requires 3D Secure verification:

1. You'll receive a push notification and a message in New Expensify asking you to review the transaction.
2. Open the New Expensify app on your mobile device.
3. Review the transaction details, including the amount, merchant, and card used.
4. Tap **Approve** to authorize the transaction, or **Deny** to cancel it.

If you approve, you may need to confirm your identity using biometrics (such as Face ID or fingerprint), depending on your device.

**Note:** You must respond within a few minutes or the transaction will fail automatically.

# How to fix a failed 3D Secure transaction for your Expensify Card

If your Expensify Card transaction was declined or failed during 3D Secure verification, check the following:

1. **Confirm New Expensify is installed** — 3D Secure challenges are delivered through the New Expensify mobile app. If the app isn't installed, the verification request can't reach you and the transaction will fail.
2. **Confirm push notifications are enabled** — Open your device settings and make sure notifications are turned on for New Expensify. Without push notifications, you won't see the approval request in time.
3. **Confirm you're signed in** — Make sure you're signed in to the New Expensify app on your mobile device. If you're signed out, the verification request won't be delivered.
4. **Check the response time window** — 3D Secure requests expire after a few minutes. If you didn't respond in time, the transaction will have failed automatically. Try the purchase again and respond to the prompt as soon as it appears.

If you've confirmed all of the above and your Expensify Card is still being declined, contact Concierge for help.

# How to update the phone number linked to your Expensify Card

Your phone number in Expensify is synced to the card provider and may be used by your bank for its own SMS verification. To make sure any verification codes go to the right number, keep your phone number up to date.

**On web:**

1. Click **Settings** in the navigation tabs on the left.
2. Click your name at the top.
3. Click **Phone number** and enter your updated number.

**On mobile:**

1. Tap your profile picture in the navigation tabs at the bottom.
2. Tap **Phone number**.
3. Enter your updated number.

Your new phone number will sync to the card provider automatically.

# FAQ

## Why didn't I receive a 3D Secure verification prompt?

3D Secure challenges are delivered through the New Expensify mobile app, not by SMS or email. You need the app installed with push notifications enabled, and you must be signed in. If any of these aren't set up, the request won't reach you and the transaction will fail. See the troubleshooting steps above.

## I'm receiving SMS codes at an old phone number — how do I fix this?

Some banks send their own SMS verification codes separate from Expensify's in-app 3D Secure flow. These codes go to the phone number linked to your card profile. Update your phone number in Expensify (see steps above) and it will sync to the card provider automatically. If codes still go to the wrong number after updating, contact Concierge for help.

## What's the difference between the in-app prompt and an SMS verification code?

Expensify's 3D Secure prompt appears directly in the New Expensify mobile app — you approve or deny the transaction with a tap and no SMS is involved. Some banks and merchants may also send their own SMS codes as part of a separate process, using the phone number linked to your card profile.

## What happens if I deny a 3D Secure transaction?

The transaction is cancelled immediately. The merchant will see it as declined, and no charge will be applied to your Expensify Card.

## Can I complete 3D Secure verification on a desktop or web browser?

3D Secure challenges for the Expensify Card are delivered through the mobile app. You'll need the New Expensify app on your phone to approve or deny the transaction.

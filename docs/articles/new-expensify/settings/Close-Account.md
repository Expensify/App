Closing your Expensify account will permanently delete associated personal data. However, expenses shared with others, such as approved or reimbursed company expenses, will remain visible to those users. Expensify may also retain certain records to comply with legal requirements.

---

## Who can close their Expensify Account 

Before you can close your Expensify account, you'll want to confirm that: 

 - You are not the Billing Owner for workspaces that are shared with other users. Learn how to confirm the Workspace Billing Owner[link], Transfer Billing Ownership[link] or Delete a Workspace[link].
 -You do not have an outstanding balance in Expensify. Learn how to Clear your billing balance[link].
 - You are not the sole Domain Admin on your Domain. Learn how to [Add a Domian Admin](https://help.expensify.com/articles/new-expensify/domains/Domain-Admins#how-to-add-a-domain-admin).
   
---

## How to close Your Expensify account

1. In the navigation tabs (on the left on web, and at the bottom on mobile), click **Account > Security**.
2. Under **Security options**, click **Close account**.
3. Follow the prompts and answer the required questions.
4. Click **Close Account** to confirm.

# FAQ 

## How do I fix a locked bank account so my account can be closed? 

If a Business Bank Account you own is locked in Expensify, you will need to unlock the bank account before proceeding with closing your Expensify account. Learn how to [Unlock a Business Bank Account](https://help.expensify.com/articles/new-expensify/wallet-and-payments/Unlock-a-Business-Bank-Account#why-is-the-business-bank-account-locked). 

## How do I validate my Expensify account so that it can be closed? 

Only validated Expensify accounts can be closed. To validate your Expensify account: 

1. [Log out of Expensify](https://expensify.com/signout?clean=true).
2. On a web or mobile browser, sign in at https://www.expensify.com/.

When you've logged back into Expensify, your account will be validated. 

## What do I do if my account can;t be closed due to being a domain member? 

If you are a member of a verified domain, a Domain Admin on your company's domain will need to close your account for you. 

To reach a Domain Admin, Ask your workspace's Billing Owner or the person who usually approves your expense reports — they’ll typically know who the Domain Admins are or be one themselves.

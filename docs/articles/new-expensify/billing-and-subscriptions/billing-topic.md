# How to resolve billing issues. 

Subscription billing problems occur when Expensify can't successfully charge the billing owner's payment method or when another billing issue requires attention. Only the billing owner receives these alerts and can resolve them.

Billing alerts are available on the web only. Subscription management isn't available in the mobile app.

---

## Who receives subscription billing alerts in New Expensify

Only the billing owner receives subscription billing alerts and can take action to resolve them.

Workspace Admins and other members don't receive these alerts or have access to subscription payment settings.

---

## Where to find subscription billing alerts in New Expensify

When a billing problem occurs, you'll see it in two places:

* A red indicator on your account menu.
* A billing banner at the top of **Account > Subscription**.

The billing banner explains the problem and provides the action needed to resolve it.

---

## What subscription billing messages mean in New Expensify

Depending on the issue, you may see messages indicating:

* Your payment card couldn't be charged.
* Your payment card has expired or is expiring soon.
* Your payment requires authentication.
* Your payment information is outdated.
* Your payment is past due.
* A billing dispute is preventing payment.

Each message includes the action needed to resolve the problem.

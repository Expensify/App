# Understand why billing issues occur

Subscription billing problems occur when Expensify can't successfully charge the billing owner's payment method or when another subscription billing issue requires attention. When this happens, Expensify displays a subscription billing alert for the billing owner.

Subscription billing alerts are available on the web only. Subscription management isn't available in the mobile app.

---

## Who receives subscription billing alerts

Only the billing owner receives subscription billing alerts and can take action to resolve subscription billing problems.

Workspace Admins and other members don't receive subscription billing alerts or have access to subscription payment settings.

---

## Where to find subscription billing alerts

Subscription billing alerts appear whenever the billing owner needs to take action to keep subscription billing active.

When a subscription billing problem occurs, you'll see the alert in two places:

 - A red indicator on your account menu.
 - A billing banner at the top of **Account > Subscription**.

The billing banner explains the billing problem and the action required to resolve it.

---

## What subscription billing messages mean 

Depending on the billing problem, you may see messages indicating:

 - Your payment failed because your payment card couldn't be charged.
 - Your payment card has expired or is expiring soon.
 - Your payment requires authentication, such as 3D Secure verification.
 - Your payment information is outdated.
 - Your subscription payment is past due.
 - A billing dispute is preventing payment.

Each billing message explains the action required to resolve that specific subscription billing problem.

## What happens after a subscription billing problem occurs

A subscription billing alert remains visible until the billing owner resolves the billing problem.

Once payment succeeds or the required action is completed, the subscription billing alert is removed and subscription billing continues normally.

# Manage Personal Karma and Corporate Karma

Personal Karma and Corporate Karma let you automatically donate to Expensify.org based on qualifying spending. Personal Karma is based on the expenses you create in your own account, while Corporate Karma is based on approved company spending across your workspaces. Although both programs use the same donation model, they're managed separately and donations are calculated independently for each program.

Learn more about [Expensify.org](https://www.expensify.org/about).

---

## Who can manage Personal Karma and Corporate Karma

Any member can manage Personal Karma for their own account.

Only the workspace owner can manage Corporate Karma because donations are charged to the payment card used for the Expensify subscription.

Before you can enable either program, you must have a valid payment card on file. To learn how to add one, see [Add or Update a Payment Card](/articles/new-expensify/billing-and-subscriptions/manage-your-subscription-and-billing/manage-billing/Add-or-Update-a-Payment-Card).

---

## How to enable or disable Personal Karma

1. In the navigation tabs (on the left on web, on the bottom on mobile), go to **Account**.
2. Select **Save the world**.
3. In the **Enable Personal Karma** section, turn Personal Karma on or off.
4. Add a payment card if prompted.
   
---

## How to manage Corporate Karma

Corporate Karma is managed in Expensify Classic and can't be configured in New Expensify. To enable or disable Corporate Karma, see [Setting Up Karma Donations](/articles/expensify-classic/expensify-billing/Personal-and-Corporate-Karma#setting-up-corporate-karma-donations).

---

## What happens after you enable Personal Karma or Corporate Karma

Both Personal Karma and Corporate Karma use the same donation model. For every **$500** in qualifying spending, **$1** is donated to Expensify.org. Donation totals are calculated separately for each program at the end of every month.

Every donation is automatically assigned to an Expensify.org fund based on the Merchant Category Code (MCC) associated with each qualifying expense. You can't choose which fund receives your donation.

Expensify.org supports five fund categories:

- Climate Justice
- Food Security
- Housing Equity
- Reentry Services
- Youth Advocacy

To learn more about these funds, visit [Expensify.org Funding](https://www.expensify.org/funds).

After the monthly donation is calculated, Expensify charges the payment card associated with the enabled Karma program. The donation is charged separately from your subscription, and you'll receive a donation receipt after each successful charge.

# FAQ

## Why am I being charged for a Karma donation?

Personal Karma or Corporate Karma is enabled and a monthly donation was calculated from your qualifying spending. Turn off the applicable Karma program to prevent future donations.

## Will turning off Karma cancel donations that have already been charged?

No. Turning off Personal Karma or Corporate Karma prevents future donations. Donations that have already been processed aren't affected.

## Why do my Personal Karma and Corporate Karma donations have different amounts?

Personal Karma is calculated from your personal qualifying spending, while Corporate Karma is calculated from approved company spending across your workspaces. Because they're calculated independently, the donation amounts may differ.

## When am I charged for Karma donations?

Donation totals are calculated at the end of each month. If a donation is due, it's charged to the associated payment card separately from your subscription.

## Do Personal Karma or Corporate Karma affect my subscription pricing?

No. Donations are charged separately from your subscription.

## Can I make recurring donations without using Personal Karma or Corporate Karma?

Yes. You can become an Expensify.org member and make a recurring monthly donation that's separate from Personal Karma and Corporate Karma. Learn more at [Donate to Expensify.org](https://www.expensify.org/donate).
